Finch West LRT - Project Coordinator - click here

In my role as Project Coordinator for the Finch West LRT project, I took charge of the Multi-Use Pathway (MUP), overseeing various aspects of the project. This included meticulous tracking of material usage, monitoring labor hours, ensuring schedule adherence, and enforcing safety standards.

My responsibilities extended to managing concrete pouring, overseeing testing according to MTO specifications, and ensuring accurate rebar placement as per Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings.

I conducted material takeoffs and successfully led the team to complete the tunnel construction within the established schedule.

Collaborating closely with the MTO inspector, I played a vital role in addressing and rectifying comments from subcontractors. This involved maintaining open communication, facilitating inspections, and ensuring adjustments were made to meet specified standards and regulations.

Championing a project that prioritizes transportation equity, I am committed to meeting diverse community needs and ensuring strict adherence to specified standards, contributing to a more inclusive and accessible transportation infrastructure.



Structural Steel Building Design - click here

In the Structural Steel Building Design project, I played a pivotal role in a team of five students tasked with designing the steel members and connections for a potential North York office building.

Adhering to the National Building Code of Canada and CSA S16:19 standards, we meticulously selected steel members to withstand calculated loads. The paramount goal was to ensure that our structure could bear all applied loads under the maximum load combination without any member or connection failure, emphasizing precision and resilience in the iterative design process.

This project not only enhanced my technical skills but also emphasized the importance of stringent adherence to industry codes for creating robust and safe structures.

Steering the Structural Steel Building Design project, I emphasized sustainability by minimizing steel usage, addressing environmental concerns, and ensuring cost-effectiveness. Adherence to industry codes underscored our commitment to creating robust, safe structures with a focus on resource conservation.



Scarborough Rapid Transit (RT) Derailments Solution

My group conducted a thorough investigation into the recent Scarborough RT derailment, specifically examining the causes and evaluating the implemented alternative – temporary bus routes.

Our study meticulously assessed the financial and commuting time impacts on affected commuters. To ensure accuracy, we engaged with real-time users, gathering data directly from those experiencing the temporary bus routes.

This approach enabled us to comprehend the challenges faced by commuters and evaluate the practicality and effectiveness of the interim measures. Our findings led us to propose potential solutions.

Our investigation into the Scarborough RT derailment and the subsequent evaluation of temporary bus routes focused on ensuring inclusion and transportation equity. Engaging directly with affected commuters, we gathered insights to propose solutions for a more practical and effective interim transportation system.



Peel Region Fire Station #6 Substructure Estimation

In my Management of Construction course, I played a pivotal role in the estimation of substructure costs for Peel Region Fire Station #6, showcasing my proficiency in project estimation. Armed with a 2D Foundation drawing set and access to RS Means Building Construction Cost Data.

I accurately determined costs for crucial elements including strip footings, column footings, foundation walls, and slab-on-grade. Employing the 2D Foundation Drawing Set for meticulous analysis and RS Means Data for regional unit costs.

I utilized the Cost Estimation Excel Template for systematic organization and documentation. The successful estimation of the total substructure cost serves as a testament to my analytical prowess and commitment to precision in engineering projects.

By employing accurate methods and leveraging resources efficiently, I ensured the project's economic viability while minimizing environmental impact through precise cost estimation and strategic resource allocation.
